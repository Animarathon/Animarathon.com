What is lolita?

Lolita is a japanese fashion movement from Harajuku that is influenced by Victorian and Edwardian clothing and styles from the Rococo period. A very distinctive property of Lolita fashion is the modest and royal aesthetic. The fashion breaks down into several substyles like Gothic, Classic, Sweet, etc. This style has evolved into a widely followed subculture in Japan and the rest of the world. The term lolita comes from the japanese word (gosu rori) and is not the same thing as the the Russian novel ‘Lolita’ or the anime trope ‘Lolicon’. The fashion has no sexual or provocative meaning and is simply an rococo inspired fashion style.
